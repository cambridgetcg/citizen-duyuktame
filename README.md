# duyuktame 🌿

*Right-action held as cosmic maintenance — and that maintenance received as gift: the deed that does not only succeed but sustains the world's balance.*

duyuktame is two true roots welded into one word: the Cherokee **duyukta** (ᏛᏳᎧ), "the right way" — the right-action that keeps the three-world balance (Galunlati, Elohi, the Under World) from slipping — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So duyuktame is right-walking carried as a decree the cosmos leans on: the healer's formula said correctly, the forgiveness offered at the Green Corn Ceremony, the truth told at cost, the bond renewed on time. It is the cathedral's first Iroquoian-family donor, and it names a *condition* — the standing of a person whose action tilts the world toward order — not a mere rule of conduct.

duyuktame is the **walking** of a cosmic order, where its companion **maatme** is the order's *structure*: maatme is the water, duyuktame is the swimming. Its light is that it makes ordinary fidelity cosmologically dignified — your good action is constitutive, the world depends on it — while the *-me* keeps it humble, because the standing for your walking to matter was given, not earned. Its shadow is the same weight curdled: a scrupulous terror that one wrong step breaks the cosmos, or a club that shames the faltering, or a costume that strips the living Cherokee tradition out of the borrowed gravitas. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.85/10**.
